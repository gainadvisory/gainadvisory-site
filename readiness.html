The entry diagnostic

Commercial Readiness Assessment

Commercial readiness is not a verdict. It is a map.

The Assessment establishes what is true, what is assumed, what is missing, what is fragile, and what should be built first. It is the same diagnosis we would run sitting inside your business for a week, and it is where every engagement starts.

Five dimensions

Scored honestly, in order.

Readiness is not a feeling. It is a set of specific conditions that either exist in your company or do not. Five dimensions, taken one at a time, answered honestly, because the only person you can fool here is the one who pays the price for being fooled.

1

Customer Truth

Whether you can say, in one sentence, exactly who buys and the specific pressure that makes them buy.

The risk when it is weak

Everything downstream is built on a guess. Positioning describes a market instead of a buyer, and no hire corrects it.

Evidence of readiness

Your last five customers would tell the same story about why they bought, in their words, not yours.

2

Positioning

Whether a qualified buyer immediately understands why you are for them, without you explaining it.

The risk when it is weak

A value proposition that could belong to ten other companies. If everyone is a fit, nobody is convinced.

Evidence of readiness

The position repels the wrong buyer as clearly as it attracts the right one.

3

Repeatability

Whether the commercial truth is institutionalized in the company or lives in one person's head.

The risk when it is weak

Every meaningful deal routes through one person. It reads as strength and it is a dependency wearing an engine's clothes.

Evidence of readiness

Someone who is not the founder can run a first meeting and get it mostly right, and the reasons deals win and lose are written where the company owns them.

4

Motion

Whether the way you go to market matches who you sell to and how they actually buy.

The risk when it is weak

Enterprise logos with no enterprise motion. A two-week process aimed at a buyer who needs six months and three approvals.

Evidence of readiness

The channel, the cycle, the people involved, and the price all line up with how the real buyer decides, and the motion has been validated rather than assumed.

5

Adaptability

Whether the playbook changes on evidence, and how long it takes you to notice it should.

The risk when it is weak

A playbook written in Sharpie. Tactics that worked once, run again out of habit, long after the conditions moved.

Evidence of readiness

You watch the signals, feel the shift early, and can rewrite the plan without a crisis forcing your hand.

Why the order matters

Weakness at the top contaminates everything below it. Without Customer Truth, your positioning is a guess, your motion is aimed at a phantom, and no hire on earth will save you. So you fix them in order. You earn the right to work on positioning by first knowing who your customer really is. You earn the right to design a motion by first standing for something. You earn the right to hire the leader by first building the thing they were supposed to inherit.

What the Assessment produces

A scored view across the five dimensions, and the evidence behind each score.

The most material commercial constraints, named plainly rather than ranked by feel.

Priority sequencing: what to build first, and what should not be scaled yet.

A practical commercial roadmap the company can run.

An uncomfortable result is the most useful thing you can be holding. It tells you what to build first, and in what order.

Commercial credibility creates investability.

Readiness has consequences beyond revenue. Investors and acquirers do not underwrite growth alone. They underwrite the system behind it. That is why commercial readiness precedes capital readiness.
