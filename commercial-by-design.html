The central problem

A company cannot scale what only a few people know.

Early revenue proves that someone in the company can sell. It does not prove the company can.

Commercial by Design is about the work between those two conditions: discovering how the company actually wins, testing it in the market, and transferring the learning into a capability the organization owns.

Inside the playbook

Four disciplines.
One learning engine.

The playbook follows the path from assumptions to organizational capability, without pretending commercial growth is a linear checklist.

01

Diagnose

Replace inherited assumptions with evidence about who buys, why they buy, and what actually opens the budget.

→ Commercial Truth
02

Design

Turn what is true into an explicit, connected commercial motion the organization can test.

→ Commercial Architecture
03

Build

Put the architecture into the market, learn from what happens, and let evidence earn every change.

→ Market Evidence
04

Transfer

Move validated learning out of individual heads and into a system the company can own.

→ Organizational Capability

The method · D²BT

Not a sequence of steps.
An engine that learns.

The disciplines begin in sequence. They do not remain linear. Execution produces evidence, evidence improves the architecture, and validated learning transfers continuously into the organization.
